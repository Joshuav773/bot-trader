## 📚 BLACK-SCHOLES-MERTON FORMULAS USED

**Call Option Pricing:**
$$C = S_0 N(d_1) - Ke^{-rT}N(d_2)$$

**Put Option Pricing:**
$$P = Ke^{-rT}N(-d_2) - S_0 N(-d_1)$$

**Where:**
$$d_1 = \frac{\ln(S_0/K) + (r + \sigma^2/2)T}{\sigma\sqrt{T}}$$

$$d_2 = d_1 - \sigma\sqrt{T}$$

**Parameters:**
- $S_0$ = Current stock price ($57.41)
- $K$ = Strike price
- $T$ = Time to expiration (0.0055 years)
- $r$ = Risk-free rate (0.045 or 4.5%)
- $\sigma$ = Implied volatility
- $N(\cdot)$ = Cumulative normal distribution

**Greeks:**
- Delta: $N(d_1)$ for calls, $-N(-d_1)$ for puts
- Gamma: $\frac{N'(d_1)}{S_0 \sigma \sqrt{T}}$
- Vega: $S_0 N'(d_1) \sqrt{T}$
- Theta: Time decay per day
